05
Signature recipes
The exact method that makes your food stand out
KFC-Style Crispy Fried Chicken
Serves 4 · 8 pieces · The two secrets: overnight marination and double-coating
Ingredients
  • Chicken pieces8 pcs
  • Plain flour2 cups
  • Cornflour4 tbsp
  • Eggs2
  • Milk or water½ cup
  • Garlic powder2 tsp
  • Onion powder2 tsp
  • Paprika2 tsp
  • White + black pepper1 tsp each
  • Cayenne pepper½ tsp
  • Dried thyme + oregano1 tsp each
  • Salt2 tsp
  • Chicken stock cube1
  • Cooking oil1 litre
Steps
  1. Marinate overnight: Score chicken deeply. Mix all spices + stock cube. Rub all over. Refrigerate 4+ hours.
  2. Make coating: Mix flour + cornflour + spices. Whisk eggs + milk separately.
  3. Double coat: Egg wash → flour → egg wash → flour. Press hard to create craggy clumps.
  4. Rest 15 mins: Let coated pieces rest uncovered — dries the coating.
  5. Fry medium heat: 3–4 pieces at a time, 12–15 mins. Deep golden brown. Rest 5 mins before serving.
CJ's-Style Loaded Fries
Serves 4 · The double-fry method is everything
Ingredients
  • Large potatoes~1 kg
  • Cooking oil500ml
  • Salt1.5 tsp
  • Garlic powder1 tsp
  • Mayonnaise4 tbsp
  • Tomato ketchup3 tbsp
  • Chili sauce / piri piri2 tbsp
  • Garlic minced1 tsp
  • Soy sauce1 tsp
  • Shredded chicken or beef100g
  • Onion + tomatoes1 + 2
  • Cheese50g
  • Spring onions2
Steps
  1. Soak 30 mins: Cut potatoes into thick strips. Soak in cold salted water. Drain and pat dry.
  2. First fry: Medium heat, 4 minutes — cooked but NOT golden. Cool completely.
  3. Make house sauce: Mix mayo + ketchup + chili + garlic + soy sauce. This is your secret signature.
  4. Second fry: High heat, 3–4 mins until deeply golden. Season immediately.
  5. Load and serve: Fries → sauce → meat → cheese → spring onions. Serve immediately.

08
Risks & how to handle them
Every risk you will face — and what to do about it

High probability risks

High risk
Slow first weeks
Keep 100k emergency float. Do not quit before Week 4.
High risk
Rain and bad weather cutting customers by 70%
Budget 15k–25k for a tarpaulin canopy from Week 1.
High risk
Running out of ingredients during peak hours
Always prep 20% more than your expected daily sales.
High risk
Inconsistent taste losing repeat customers
Never compromise on quality to save money. Raise prices if costs increase.

Medium probability risks

Medium risk
Council fees and permits
Ask nearby vendors about fees BEFORE choosing your spot.
Medium risk
Theft of daily cash
Use Mobile Money — bank earnings daily via MTN or Airtel.
Medium risk
Copycat vendors copying your signature items
Keep your sauce recipe secret. Keep innovating.
Medium risk
Personal illness — you are the business
Train one trusted backup person who knows the full recipes.

Lower but serious risks

Lower risk
Location loss — asked to move
Never invest heavily in a fixed setup until you own or lease the spot.
Lower risk
Food spoilage in Kampala heat
Buy fresh daily — never over-prep perishables.

10
Marketing your business
Free and low-cost ways to fill your stall

Free marketing — start Day 1

  • WhatsApp status every morning — photo of food, price, and location
  • WhatsApp Business account — catalogue feature to display your full menu
  • Send personal messages to 50 contacts on launch day — first 10 orders get a discount
  • Post in Kampala Facebook groups — Buy and Sell, neighbourhood, campus groups
  • Feed your neighbours for free on Day 1 — they become your first marketers

Week 2–4 marketing

  • Make a menu graphic on Canva — clean with photos and prices
  • Ask every happy customer to share your number with one friend
  • Post TikTok or Instagram Reels — 30-second frying video gets huge reach for free
  • Put a small sign or banner outside your room or gate
  • Partner with boda riders — they bring orders, you give them commission or discount

Month 2 and beyond

  • List on Jumia Food or local delivery platforms — instant visibility to thousands
  • Create a loyalty card — buy 5 meals get 1 free
  • Target office lunch delivery — one office of 20 people = 20 daily orders
  • Weekly special every Friday — new item keeps people checking your status

Mindset & discipline
The only variable left is you

The food, the recipes, and the plan are all ready. The only remaining variable is you.

Discipline 1
Consistency above everything
Open at the same time every single day. Rain or shine. People build their routines around you. Miss two days and they find someone else forever.
Discipline 2
The 40% profit rule
Put 40% of every day's profit into a separate Mobile Money wallet. Never touch it. The vendors who stay poor spend their profits.
Discipline 3
Taste above everything
One bad meal loses you 5 potential customers. Never reduce quality to save money.
